Changes in the number and distribution of Sandhill cranes in the eastern Population over nearly 60 years.

In 2015, researchers at the International Crane Foundation published a paper in the Journal of Field Ornithology examining the changes in the number and distribution of Sandhill cranes in the Eastern Population. The Eastern Population of Sandhill Cranes in the United Sates are located in the Mississippi and Atlantic flyways and are roughly bounded by the Mississippi river on the west and the Atlantic ocean in the east. The research paper used data from annual bird surveys to help grasp the substantial changes in the number of Sandhill cranes in the population from 1965 through 2012.

One of the sources of data for this paper was the Christmas Bird Count coordinated annually by the National Audubon Society. These data, collected annually by local volunteer birders provide a wealth of data to researchers. In addition to helping show the substantial recovery of a, once, seldom seen species, the data also shows changes in the wintering locations of these birds as population density, habitat, and climate changes over time.

Average Latitude of Sandhill Cranes Recorded in Christmas Bird Count (1965 - 2013)

The changes in the avereage latitude for Sandhills in late December and early January reflects changes in the species population, changing habitat and food sources, and a change in the climate during the same period. The northern trend of the avereage latitude (orange line) over time is accompanied by en envelope (blue area) showing the core of the population's spread across different latitudes.
