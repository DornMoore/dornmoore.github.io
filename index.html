ABOUT ME

Conservation GIS and Cartography

Dorn Moore

I've been a geographer with a focus on using geospatial tools to strengthen environmental conservation around the world for 20 years. The majority of that time, I've worked with the International Crane Foundation enabling our team to ensure a future for the world's 15 species of cranes.

In the past few years I've been working on getting a second Masters degree, again in Geography, specifically on updating my expertise in the field for web mapping and coding. So much ahs changed in recent years.

When I was at the University of Arizona in 1996, I took a class on Remote Sensing; It was a turning point. I had finished my undergraduate work with a focus in Anthropology and Spanish, and I was beginning my first semester as a graduate student working towards a Master's degree in Latin American Studies focusing on environmental conservation issues. The Remote Sensing course had been a filler of a course that I figured I could learn something new and fulfill a geography requirement.

Suddenly I had discovered this other world of data and technology inside the deeper field of geography. I was lucky enough to have an academic advisor who said, "The problem is that you are a geographer that happens to be interested in Latin America." She helped me switch into the Geography program and the rest is history.
