ABOUT ME

Conservation GIS and Cartography

Dorn Moore

I've been a geographer with a focus on using geospatial tools for environmental conservation around the world for 20 years. The majority of that time, I've worked with the International Crane Foundation to ensure a future for the world's 15 species of cranes.

In the fall of 2016, I decided that I needed to catch up with the changes that happened in GIS techniques since I finished my MA in 1998. I've been working on getting a second Masters degree, again in Geography, through the University of Wisconsin's Online Professional MS in GIS and Web Map Programming. I am constantly amazed at the changes over the past 20 years.

When I was at the University of Arizona in 1996, I took a class on Remote Sensing; It was a turning point. I had finished my undergraduate work with a focus in Anthropology and Spanish, and I was beginning my first semester as a graduate student working towards a Master's degree in Latin American Studies focusing on environmental conservation issues. The Remote Sensing course had been a filler course where I could learn something new and fulfill a geography requirement.

Suddenly I had discovered this other world of data and technology inside the deeper field of geography. Further, I had a knack for it. The gis and data relationships came naturally for me. I was lucky enough to have an academic advisor who helped me switch into the Geography program and the rest is history.
